\section{Conclusion and Future Work}
In this paper, we introduced Semantic-Balanced Group Relative Policy Optimization (SB-GRPO), a novel reinforcement learning framework designed to overcome the critical bottlenecks of advantage vanishing and correction bias in LLM reasoning. By synthesizing a Heuristic Confidence Gate with Semantic Error Profiling and an Information-Theoretic Loss Scaling factor, SB-GRPO dynamically stabilizes gradients across highly skewed batches while actively targeting deep-seated logical flaws. Our empirical evaluations demonstrate that this architecture achieves state-of-the-art performance on complex mathematical benchmarks, driving unprecedented single-digit to double-digit accuracy gains on AIME 2024 and yielding substantial improvements on the MATH-500 dataset under strict hardware and parameter constraints (4-bit QLoRA \cite{hu2021loralowrankadaptationlarge,dettmers2023qloraefficientfinetuningquantized}). 

Notably, SB-GRPO also establishes a mathematically rigorous and highly efficient paradigm for aligning LLMs, proving that targeted semantic error correction is far more effective than brute-force computational scaling. Notably, SB-GRPO also sub-category analysis highlights that SB-GRPO emerges as the optimal choice for complex reasoning problems, specifically excelling in the \textit{Precalculus} and \textit{Intermediate Algebra} sub-categories, significantly outperforming existing baselines in these domains. 
Future work will explore extending this entropy-aware balancing mechanism to multi-modal reasoning tasks and dynamic length-scaling environments, further generalizing the framework's ability to self-correct across diverse abstract domains.
